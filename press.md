<p>
  <strong>Livable CRD</strong> is happy to help journalists and media covering
  livability, housing, transit, climate, and local democracy in the Capital
  Regional District. We are a non-partisan voter-education coalition: we inform,
  and we do not endorse candidates.
</p>

<h2>Media contact</h2>
<p>
  For all press inquiries, please email
  <a href="mailto:{{ site.email }}?subject=Press%20inquiry">{{ site.email }}</a>.
  We aim to respond within two business days. If you are working on a tight
  deadline, note it in your subject line and we will do our best to prioritize.
</p>

<h2>What to include</h2>
<p>
  To help us get you what you need quickly, please include:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>Your name, outlet, and role.</li>
  <li>A short description of your story and the angle you are exploring.</li>
  <li>Any specific questions, topics, or municipalities you want to focus on.</li>
  <li>Your deadline and preferred format (written statement, interview, or background).</li>
</ul>

<h2>Interviews and background</h2>
<p>
  Coalition spokespeople are available for interviews and background
  conversations about our methodology, the topics we evaluate, and what the
  scorecard means for voters. Because our grades come from a coalition of
  organizations, we can also point you to the partner best placed to speak on a
  given policy area.
</p>

<h2>About Livable CRD</h2>
<p>
  Livable CRD is a non-partisan coalition of community groups preparing a
  candidate scorecard for municipal elections across the Capital Regional
  District of British Columbia. Participating organizations collaborate on a
  shared questionnaire covering transit, housing, arts, climate, walking,
  cycling, youth, healthcare, and general livability, then grade candidates'
  responses using a published, reproducible methodology. Read more
  <a href="{{ '/about/' | relative_url }}">about the coalition</a>.
</p>
