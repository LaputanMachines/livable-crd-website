**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Victoria, BC, July 22, 2026** — A coalition of community organizations from
across the Capital Regional District today announced the launch of Livable CRD,
a non-partisan voter-engagement project. The group will publish a candidate
scorecard and questionnaire for the region's municipal elections, with the goal
of increasing voter turnout and civic engagement with easy to understand
information and resources. The scorecard grades candidates on their positions on
issues that shape daily life across the region: transit, housing, arts, climate,
walking, cycling, healthcare, and good governance. If voters prefer, they can
instead just look at the questionnaire rather than relying on grades.

Livable CRD does not endorse candidates. Instead, it publishes candidates'
positions in a clear, comparable format so residents can make informed choices
at the ballot box.

"We keep hearing from people that when they vote in municipal elections that
it's really hard to find out where candidates actually stand on issues," said
Ray Oakley, a Livable CRD spokesperson. "We are not telling anyone how to vote.
We are doing the legwork so that every resident can compare candidates side by
side, in plain language, before election day." Michael Bassili, another
spokesperson for Livable CRD, added, "it's nearly impossible to figure out who
to vote for and why, even with all-candidate debates, because there are just so
many of them. We intend to solve that."

## How the scorecard works

Participating organizations collaborate on a shared questionnaire, with each
group contributing questions in its area of expertise. Livable CRD then sends
this questionnaire to candidates, and their responses are graded with a
published, reproducible methodology using a clear letter-grade system. Where a
candidate declines to respond, that is documented as well. Results will be
organized by municipality so voters can focus on the races on their own ballot.

## A growing coalition

Livable CRD brings together advocates from across the region, including Homes
for Living, Walk On Victoria, Better Island Transit, Climate Justice Victoria,
Family Doctors for Saanich, Better Transit YYJ, and Victori'us Arts. The
coalition continues to grow, and prospective member organizations are welcome to
reach out.

## What comes next

The coalition is finalizing its questionnaire and methodology now. Candidate
surveys, per-municipality pages, and full candidate profiles with letter grades
will be published ahead of election day. Progress updates will appear on the
[Livable CRD blog]({{ '/blog/' | relative_url }}), and residents can
[subscribe to the mailing list]({{ '/signup/' | relative_url }}) to follow along.

## About Livable CRD

Livable CRD is a non-partisan coalition of community groups preparing a candidate
scorecard for municipal elections across the Capital Regional District of British
Columbia. Our mission is to make voting easier by providing clear, accessible
information that empowers more people to participate in local elections. The
coalition informs; it does not endorse. Learn more at
[livablecrd.ca]({{ '/' | relative_url }}) or read more
[about the coalition]({{ '/about/' | relative_url }}).
